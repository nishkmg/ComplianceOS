Security Policy

A definitive overview of the encryption protocols, data residency mandates, and operational security frameworks employed by ComplianceOS to safeguard Indian enterprise ledger data.

Effective Date: October 1, 2024

Version: 2.1.4

1. Data Encryption Standards

ComplianceOS mandates rigorous cryptographic standards across all data states. We recognize that financial ledger data constitutes critical infrastructure for our clients, demanding uncompromising protective measures.

enhanced_encryption In-Transit & At-Rest Protocols

  • check_circle
    Transport Layer Security (TLS 1.3) All communication between client applications and our API infrastructure is secured exclusively via TLS 1.3, ensuring perfect forward secrecy and mitigating protocol downgrade attacks.
  • check_circle
    AES-256 Encryption Ledger data residing within our databases, backups, and secondary storage systems is symmetrically encrypted using Advanced Encryption Standard (AES) with 256-bit keys.

2. Data Residency & Sovereignty

In strict adherence to RBI guidelines and the Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP), ComplianceOS maintains absolute data residency within the sovereign borders of India.

We operate out of Tier-IV data centers located exclusively in Mumbai and Chennai. At no point is client ledger data processed, mirrored, or stored on servers located outside Indian jurisdiction.

Continuous audit trails verify geographic data confinement, accessible via the ComplianceOS Ledger Tools module.

Primary DC: Mumbai Region (ap-south-1)
