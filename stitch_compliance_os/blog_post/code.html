Navigating Section 43B(h): A New Era for MSME Payments in India

Ananya Sharma, FCA

Principal Consultant, ComplianceOS

For the Indian chartered accountant, few amendments in recent memory have sparked as much urgent debate as the introduction of clause (h) to Section 43B of the Income Tax Act. It is a legislative lever designed to solve a perennial problem: the delayed payment cycle that stifles Micro and Small Enterprises (MSMEs).

The 45-Day Mandate

The crux of the matter lies in timing. Under the new regime, any sum payable by an assessee to a registered MSME must be cleared within the time limit specified in Section 15 of the MSMED Act, 2006. If no agreement exists, this is 15 days; if an agreement exists, it cannot exceed 45 days. Failure to comply results in the expense being disallowed for that financial year, only to be claimed in the year of actual payment.

Audit Trail Requirements

During an audit, the burden of proof rests on the assessee. You must maintain a rigorous ledger that identifies:

  • check_circle Automated age-analysis reports specifically tuned to the 15/45-day thresholds.
  • check_circle Real-time alerts for payments approaching the disallowance deadline.

As we move deeper into the fiscal year, the pressure on CFOs to reconcile these balances will only intensify. The editorial ledger is no longer just a metaphor; it is the blueprint for modern Indian fiscal survival.
